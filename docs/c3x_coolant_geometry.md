## Kumar 2023 Paper Reconstruction Correction

The UV-frame downstream baseline above is retained only as an iteration record.
It must not be used as the paper-reproduction geometry. A source audit on
2026-06-27 found three independent problems:

- NASA Table II point 29 was transcribed as `y=4.115 mm`; the source value is
  `0.0411 cm = 0.411 mm`.
- The old row locations came from a fitted Fig. 8 U/V transform. NASA CR-182133
  instead defines the downstream pair centers by surface arc: `25.2%` on the
  suction surface and `22.5%` on the pressure surface, with `S/D=3.0`.
- NASA Table IV uses `P/D=4.0` for the chordwise row pitch and `S/D` for the
  spanwise hole pitch. The leading-edge span pitch is `7.5D=7.425 mm`; the
  downstream span pitch is `3D=2.970 mm`. Their minimum common periodic span is
  `15D=14.850 mm`, containing two LE holes and five downstream holes per row.
- SpaceClaim's `CylinderBody.Create` parameter labels are misleading. Its own
  example resolves to: first axial endpoint `A`, second axial endpoint `B`, and
  a circumference point `B+r` on the second endpoint plane. The final SAT check
  must report the full axis length, `r=0.495 mm`, zero lateral taper, and planar
  caps; body-face count alone is not accepted as validation.

The corrected source-based row positions are:

| Row | Surface | NASA row | `s/s0` | Paper case 1 angle |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|
| SS1 | suction | 12 | `0.241022565` | `35 deg` |
| SS2 | suction | 11 | `0.262977435` | `35 deg` |
| PS1 | pressure | 18 | `0.210838936` | `30 deg` |
| PS2 | pressure | 19 | `0.239161064` | `30 deg` |

These values come from NASA Table III surface arcs (`180.37 mm` suction,
`139.82 mm` pressure), pair centers (`0.252`, `0.225`), and row pitch
`P=4D=3.960 mm`. The reconstruction matches both Table III arcs within
`0.015 mm`. Cylinder directions are formed from the local surface tangent and
the Kumar forward/reverse injection definition; they are not forced to point at
the older traced plenums.

## Parallel/Length Correction and Leading Edge Baseline

The 2026-06-28 correction keeps every downstream wall intersection unchanged,
but uses one shared axis direction for both rows on each surface. Therefore
`SS1` and `SS2` are exactly parallel, and `PS1` and `PS2` are exactly parallel.
The downstream inspection cylinders now extend `3D` into the passage and `3D`

## External Flow Selection

Retain the current `c3x_kumar_case1_external_flow.scdoc` passage shape: one
NASA C3X cutout, straight inlet/outlet caps, and an exactly translated pair of
periodic side walls. This is preferable to the older long UV-frame outer field
for periodic meshing and boundary pairing. A downstream cap normal to the
nominal `-72.38 deg` exit direction would break the exact translational
periodic topology in this single-passage construction; the straight cap is the
more robust baseline for the present optimization workflow.
